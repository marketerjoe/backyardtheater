The Story

About the Show

In the kingdom of Arendelle, two sisters couldn't be more different. Elsa, the elder princess, was born with a breathtaking and terrifying gift: the power to create ice and snow with her bare hands. Fearful of hurting those she loves, she spent her childhood behind locked doors — hiding her powers from the world, and even from Anna, the little sister who adored her.

On the day of Elsa's coronation, one careless moment of emotion causes her powers to erupt in front of the entire kingdom. Exposed and overwhelmed, she flees into the frozen mountains — and accidentally plunges all of Arendelle into an eternal winter.

Anna refuses to give up on her sister. Armed with little more than optimism and stubbornness, she sets off into the icy wilderness, picking up unlikely companions along the way: Kristoff, a rugged ice harvester with a reindeer for a best friend; Sven, said reindeer; and Olaf, a warmhearted snowman who dreams, somewhat unfortunately, of experiencing summer.

Frozen is a story about the power of love — but not the kind you'd expect. It's about the bond between sisters, the courage to let people in, and the discovery that the greatest magic of all might just be the strength to be yourself.

The Cast

Characters

Every role in Frozen brings something unique to the story — from royalty to snowmen to ancient trolls. Auditions for all characters will open soon.

❄️
Elsa
The Snow Queen · Lead Role
The older princess of Arendelle, born with the power to create ice and snow. Fearful of hurting others, she isolates herself behind locked doors — until her powers are accidentally revealed at her coronation. Elsa flees to the mountains and builds her own frozen kingdom, finally free to be herself. Her journey is one of self-acceptance: learning that love is not a weakness to fear, but the only force that can truly set her free.
🌸
Anna
Princess of Arendelle · Lead Role
Elsa's younger sister: fearless, clumsy, and endlessly optimistic. Shut out from Elsa's life since childhood, Anna has never stopped believing in her sister. When Arendelle is plunged into eternal winter, she doesn't hesitate — she sets off alone into the frozen wilderness to bring Elsa home. Anna is the heart of the story, proving that true love means showing up for the people you love no matter what it costs.
🏔️
Kristoff
Ice Harvester
A rugged, no-nonsense ice harvester who reluctantly agrees to guide Anna into the frozen mountains. Gruff on the outside, fiercely loyal at heart — his unlikely friendship with Anna changes them both.
☃️
Olaf
The Snowman
A cheerful, naive snowman accidentally brought to life by Elsa's magic. He dreams with complete sincerity of experiencing summer — with no idea what that would actually mean for him. Pure comic joy.
🦌
Sven
Kristoff's Reindeer
Kristoff's devoted reindeer and best friend. Loyal, expressive, and more emotionally intelligent than his owner. Sven is the soul of the story's lighter moments.
🤴
Hans
Prince of the Southern Isles
A charming, handsome prince who sweeps Anna off her feet from the moment they meet. Attentive, kind, and seemingly perfect — though his true motivations run far darker than they first appear.
🎩
Duke of Weselton
Comic Villain
A pompous, scheming trade representative who uses Elsa's dangerous powers as a pretext to seize control of Arendelle. More bluster than brains, but dangerous in the wrong company.
🪨
Grand Pabbie
Troll Elder
The ancient, wise leader of the hidden troll colony who raised Kristoff as one of their own. He holds powerful secrets about Elsa's past — and a warning that shapes the entire story.
🛖
Oaken
Wandering Oaken's Trading Post · Supporting Role
The jolly, oversized proprietor of Wandering Oaken's Trading Post and Sauna — conveniently located in the middle of nowhere, right as the blizzard hits. Oaken is relentlessly cheerful, aggressively hospitable, and completely unbothered by the fact that his prices are outrageously high. He greets every customer with a booming "Yoo-hoo!" and takes any pushback on his pricing as a personal affront. A fan-favorite scene-stealer with big energy and even bigger laughs.
👑
Additional Roles
Ensemble & Supporting Characters
King Agnarr & Queen Iduna Elsa and Anna's parents, the beloved rulers of Arendelle. Their brief but powerful presence sets the emotional foundation of the entire story.
Bulda A warm, boisterous troll mother — the one who adopted Kristoff as a child and never quite stopped trying to find him a wife.
Trolls & Villagers The troll colony and the townspeople of Arendelle bring the world to life — filled with energy, humor, and heart. Great roles for the full ensemble.
Auditions

Audition Information

We are so excited to announce our 2026 summer production, Frozen. Auditions for this show are going to take place April 23rd and 24th from 5 to 7pm. You will need to fill out what day you need to audition in the form linked in the Production Details.

A doc will be posted here on April 10th showing your audition times and days according to the preferences stated in your form. If the time or date doesn’t match what you put in the form or you need to change your time or date please contact 801-633-2108.

For your audition you will need to prepare about 1 minute of a song in the same style as the show. You may be asked to read a few lines or sing certain things, but you do not need to prepare anything besides your one-minute audition piece.

A callback list will be posted by 9pm on April 24th. Callback will be on Saturday, April 25th from 4–7pm, where you will read a few lines (provided at callback — no need to bring lines) and sing a song of the character (specific song and sheet music will be provided on the website with the callback list). A small dance routine will also be taught at the callback.

A final cast list will be posted by April 29th.

Rehearsals will run from July 13th through July 30th. Performances will be July 31st and August 1st. Mandatory rehearsals will be July 27th through July 30th. Typical rehearsal times will be 11:00am–1:00pm, and some mandatory rehearsals may be 3 hours long and/or held later in the evening. Rehearsal locations may vary, so please check the rehearsal schedule when it is released.

Performances and mandatory rehearsals will be at: 14887 Upper Bend Drive, Herriman, Utah. If you have any questions about this process, please contact 801-633-2108.
